EXAMINATION CELL PROFILE
An autonomous examination cell (AEC) in an educational institution plays a crucial role in ensuring fair and efficient conduct of examinations. It operates independently, with its own set of rules and regulations, while adhering to the overall academic framework of the institution.
DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITES
The primary duties and responsibilities of an AEC include:
1. Examination Planning and Scheduling:
Developing and implementing academic calendars and examination schedules.
Determining the format, duration, and mode of examinations (written, oral, practical, online, etc.).
Allocating resources such as examination halls, invigilators, and materials.
2. Student Registration and Management:
Handling student enrolment and registration for examinations.
Maintaining accurate student records, including attendance and eligibility for examinations.
Issuing admit cards/hall tickets and identity cards.
3. Question Paper Management:
Preparing or coordinating the preparation of question papers, ensuring quality and confidentiality.
Setting up secure storage and distribution systems for question papers.
4. Examination Conduct and Invigilation:
Ensuring proper conduct of examinations in accordance with established rules and regulations.
Recruiting, training, and assigning invigilators to examination halls.
Preventing malpractice and maintaining discipline during examinations.
5. Answer Script Evaluation:
Collecting and securely storing answer scripts after examinations.
Appointing examiners and coordinating the evaluation process.
Ensuring fair and timely evaluation of answer scripts.
6. Result Processing and Publication:
Processing examination results accurately and efficiently.
Preparing and publishing results in various formats (mark sheets, grade cards, online portals).
Issuing transcripts and certificates to students.
7. Record Keeping and Archiving:
Maintaining comprehensive records of all examination-related activities.
Archiving examination papers, answer scripts, and results for future reference.
8. Confidentiality and Security:
Ensuring the confidentiality and security of all examination materials and data.
Implementing measures to prevent leakage of question papers and manipulation of results.
9. Grievance Handling:
Addressing student grievances and complaints related to examinations.
Providing a fair and transparent mechanism for resolving disputes.
10. Continuous Improvement:
Regularly reviewing and improving examination processes and procedures.
Incorporating feedback from students, faculty, and other stakeholders.
Staying updated on best practices in examination management.
Additional Responsibilities:
Conducting special examinations (re-examinations, supplementary examinations, etc.).
Managing examination fees and finances.
Implementing online examination systems and technologies.
Collaborating with other departments and authorities on academic matters.
Ensuring compliance with relevant government regulations and policies.
The specific duties and responsibilities of an AEC may vary depending on the size and nature of the educational institution, as well as the level of autonomy granted to it. However, the core functions remain the same: to ensure fair, efficient, and transparent conduct of examinations, uphold academic integrity, and promote student success.
